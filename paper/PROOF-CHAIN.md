## Theorem (working statement)

For every FFT-style method `M` and every problem `P` in the cyclotomic-DFT class (and adjacent compute-cost problems), `M` does not prove a lower bound on `P` improving past the existing threshold `T(P)`.

Formal definitions: `M` (FFT-style method) at PAPER §4.2.3; `P` (cyclotomic-DFT class and adjacent) at PAPER §4.3; `T(P)` at PAPER §1.8 + §4.4; cost framework at PAPER §1.2 + §4.2.1. The quantifiers above range over those formal objects.

## §3 Theorem K (companion theorem) — substrate-side σ-algebra coarsening

> **Theorem K.** Let `L = {(k, n) : 1 ≤ k < n, n ≥ 3}` carry its
> atomic σ-algebra `2^L`, let `F = {(p, q) : 1 ≤ p < q, gcd(p, q) = 1}`
> carry `2^F`, and let `R : L → F`, `R(k, n) = (k/g, n/g)` where
> `g = gcd(k, n)`, be the reduction map. Then:
>
> (i) `R⁻¹(2^F) ⊂ 2^L` is exactly the σ-algebra of fiber-constant
>     subsets. For a measurable codomain `(V, 𝒱)` with point-
>     separating σ-algebra (singletons in `𝒱`; `V = ℝ` with the
>     Borel σ-algebra suffices), a function `f : L → V` factors as
>     `f = g ∘ R` iff `f` is fiber-constant.
>
> (ii) Each of `f₁(k, n) = φ(n)/2`, `f₂(k, n) = L_n = 2n sin(π/n)`,
>      `f₃(k, n) = Δ_n = L_n²(1 − (π/n) cot(π/n))` is *not*
>      fiber-constant on `L`. Witnesses: `f₁(1, 5) = 2 ≠ 4 = f₁(3, 15)`;
>      `f₂(1, 3) = 3√3 ≠ 6 = f₂(2, 6)`; `f₃(1, 3) ≈ 10.68 ≠ 3.35 ≈ f₃(2, 6)`.
>
> (iii) Hence none of `f₁, f₂, f₃` is `R⁻¹(2^F)`-measurable;
>       equivalently, none factors through `R`. An apparatus
>       restricted to F-side data — denominator-rank, Thomae-height,
>       Stern-Brocot depth, Minkowski `?`-derivative, or any other
>       function on `F` lifted by `R^*` — cannot recover `f₁, f₂, f₃`.

Construction at [measure/FOR-BREAKFAST.md](measure/FOR-BREAKFAST.md) §K.0–§K.4.

**Scope.** Theorem K is a structural fact about reading the substrate through the reduction `R`. It does not by itself entail the working theorem. Its role is to certify that the direct K2 substrate-side observables on `L` are not erasable by passage to F-side coordinates: any candidate refutation that operates by recoding `(k, n)` as a reduced rational `p/q` and reading on `F` is forbidden from seeing the cyclotomic-ladder degree, the polygon perimeter, or the isoperimetric gap rate. Substrate-side content that does not transcribe to scalar L-observables (rotation-orbit Haar / `β(α)`; L-W null/full) is not blocked by K and is handled at §4 below.

- **Impossibility (conditional on substrate-side residuals).** Combining (a) substrate-side facts — Theorem K's σ-algebra coarsening on the integer-indexed lattice, plus the iso/-register currency-stratification at §5.2 (the three iso/ registers as substrate-side currencies in §1's transaction-cost sense, with `5π` worked overhead between rate and constant per `iso/THREE-REGISTER-SYNTHESIS.md` Claim 1 and a categorial type-gap to almost-every, supplying a substrate-side `δ > 0` instance) — (b) T4b together with the candidate transport, and (c) the endpoint commitment: an FFT-style method `M` cannot deliver a strict improvement on `T(P)` because the cost-coordinate `δ` does not zero at the bounded/unbounded coefficient boundary under the chosen operational cost-norm. ## §7 Coda — multi-measure coherence and post-impossibility reading

**Multi-measure framing of `T(P)`.** The lower-bound apparatus reads cost-of-`P` through heterogeneous measures, not a single `Ω(n log n)` shape: Morgenstern's `Ω(n log n)` bit-counting on bounded coefficients; Winograd's modular-product / CRT ledger `μ(T_P) = 2n − k` on bilinear-rational; AFW's multiplicative-complexity / cyclotomic-rational-equivalence ledger. The Morgenstern↔Ailon pair (per `paper/tables/COST-MODEL-CIRCLE-READING-TABLE.md`, with Ailon's restricted-model entropy potential paired against Morgenstern's bounded-side determinant) is the most explicit currency-stratification demonstration: same problem class (FFT), same scale (`Ω(n log n)`), two restricted models with two forced potentials — determinant on the unnormalized side, entropy on the normalized side (since determinant has modulus 1 there). Ailon enters as comparison witness here, not as a T(P) cell — `T(P)` is defined by the three lower bounds, with cost-model methodology (Schönhage–Strassen, Ailon) adjacent. Cross-currency coherence with the substrate-side reading at Theorem K is the *intended* reconciliation. The cross-currency alignment of `T(P)` (debt #9(b)) is substantially absorbed by debt #1's route-3 universality (`Z`'s universality *is* the reconciliation); the variable-precision canon re-read (debt #9(c)) remains the substantive open methodological commitment.

**Post-impossibility reading.** Once the impossibility argument earns its open debts, the four canon frameworks (Schönhage-Strassen, Morgenstern, Winograd, AFW) are read as sitting at their natural limits — not from an as-yet-undiscovered defect, but because no further descent is reachable on this substrate by these methods. Pair with the algebraic-side closure-mismatch reading at [memos/NATIVE-F-MINIMAL-DEFINITION.md](memos/NATIVE-F-MINIMAL-DEFINITION.md) as a sibling structural rhyme on the integer-vs-continuum asymmetry, not as a downstream piece of the impossibility. **Outflow: the recursion-theoretic horizon.** Three debts in the apparatus — #11(iii) channel exhaustiveness, #14 cost-norm uniformity, #15 (T6) cross-chart invariance — ask whether the impossibility lifts from its intensional form to an extensional one. Each is the same Rice-flavored question parameterized differently: by the FFT-style class (does T4b's faithfulness extend from the syntactic class of §1 above to the class of algorithms behaviorally equivalent to it?), by the cost-norm (is operational compressibility right for every behaviorally-equivalent scheme?), by the δ-coordinate (do all reasonable δ-algebras yield the same impossibility region at `T(P)`?).
