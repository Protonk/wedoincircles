## Theorem (working statement)

For every FFT-style method `M` and every problem `P` in the cyclotomic-DFT class (and adjacent compute-cost problems), `M` does not prove a lower bound on `P` improving past the existing threshold `T(P)`.

## §1 Definitions (working language)

None of the items below is a finished formal definition; each names an object the paper specifies. The methodological frame is the Coase / Cook-Reckhow / SS-vEB anchor pair: the existence of friction `δ` is one question, the algebra of friction is another, both stated inside a uniform-charge cost model. Trust-boundary discipline ([fft/PROVENANCE-AND-TRANSFERABILITY.md](fft/PROVENANCE-AND-TRANSFERABILITY.md)) governs every canon citation; nothing below imports a lower bound from a canon source as the program's content.

- **FFT-style method `M`.** Finite composition of native operations under standard composability, charged in the uniform-charge / logarithmic-measure cost model with the regularity guard on operation cost, stored precision, coefficient size, precomputed tables, and size-dependent constants paid at the same granularity (advice strings, oracle constants, and table-per-size shortcuts are out-of-class unless their construction and storage are explicitly charged inside the method). Methods select strategies adaptively from problem data; the conversion is an adaptive strategy family closed under composition (Gauss 1805's `4 × 3` vs `3 × 4` Pallas decomposition is the pre-1882 worked example per [fft/GOLDSTINE-1977-INTERPOLATION-BRIEF.md](fft/GOLDSTINE-1977-INTERPOLATION-BRIEF.md); deeper framing at [fft/FFT-SEARCH-PLAN.md](fft/FFT-SEARCH-PLAN.md)). **[Construction debt.]**
- **Cost currencies.** Multiplicative cost `μ` (cost-bearing primitive: multiplication; bilinear / rational-equivalence accounting; structure-bearing canon: Schönhage-Strassen 1971, Winograd 1978, Auslander-Feig-Winograd 1984) and additive cost `α` (cost-bearing primitive: addition; sensitive to coefficient regime; canonical structure-bearing result: Morgenstern 1973's `Ω(n log n)` bounded-coefficient additive lower bound). Currencies inside the cost model, not yet conversion strategies. Adjacent restricted-model precedent at Ailon 2013 ([fft/AILON-2013-UNITARY-FFT-LOWER-BOUND-BRIEF.md](fft/AILON-2013-UNITARY-FFT-LOWER-BOUND-BRIEF.md)) — `2 × 2` unitary layered model, matrix-entropy potential, `m ≥ (n log₂ n) / 2`; cited as adjacent prior art *and* as the canon's constructive in-canon witness for currency-stratification: Morgenstern's determinant potential cannot reach the normalized FFT (whose determinant has modulus 1), and matrix entropy is the replacement potential needed in the normalized case — currency-segregation forced by what each potential can see, not a coverage gap. Not imported as program content.
- **Threshold `T(P)`.** Current best lower bound on `P` from the canon: AFW's multiplicative-complexity threshold for cyclotomic DFTs under rational equivalence; Morgenstern's `Ω(n log n)` bounded-coefficient additive threshold; Winograd's modular-product threshold `μ(T_P) = 2n − k`. **[Construction debt:** per-problem specification across these distinct currencies and cross-currency reconciliation; see debt #9.**]**
- **Substrate.** The integer-indexed lattice `L = {(k, n) : 1 ≤ k < n, n ≥ 3}`, with reduction map `R : L → F` to the Farey set. The direct K2 substrate-side observables live on `L`; the σ-algebra coarsening of Theorem K is the load-bearing structural fact about this substrate. Substrate-side faces that do not transcribe to scalar L-observables (rotation-orbit Haar / `β(α)`; L-W null/full Lebesgue dichotomy) play pre-K2 framing roles; see §4.
- **Substrate-side iso/ currencies.** Three non-nesting measure-theoretic readings of the planar isoperimetric gap `Δ = L² − 4πA`: *rate* (asymptotic decay along a parametric family approaching a disk; canonical structure-bearing result: Hurwitz Parseval expansion `Δ_n = 4π⁴/(3n²) + O(1/n⁴)` on the inscribed regular `n`-gon family per [corners/HURWITZ-FIRST-BAND-CONCENTRATION.md](corners/HURWITZ-FIRST-BAND-CONCENTRATION.md)), *constant* (pointwise sharp inequality on a single curve; Bonnesen-strengthening per Osserman 1979 / Bonnesen 1921), *almost-every* (full-measure under a distribution on parameter space; Khintchine / Beck 1994 tradition). Currencies in outline §1.6's broader transaction-cost sense — distinct from the algorithm-side `μ`, `α` cost currencies above — with positive worked-instance overhead between rate and constant (`5π ≈ 15.7×` weaker than Bonnesen direct on the chained Sobolev → geometric route per [iso/THREE-REGISTER-SYNTHESIS.md](iso/THREE-REGISTER-SYNTHESIS.md) Claim 1) and pairwise non-inclusion witnesses on hypothesis classes (thin ellipse, small spike, categorial type-gap to Beck's `α`-parameter space). Source-side typing per [measure/SUBSTRATE-OBSTRUCTIONS.md](measure/SUBSTRATE-OBSTRUCTIONS.md) §2.
- **Transaction cost `δ`.** A non-zero quantity associated with non-nesting measure-theoretic readings of one quantity, reflecting irreducible conversion cost. The canonical algorithm-side instance is the bounded ↔ unbounded coefficient conversion between cost currencies `μ` and `α`; the canonical substrate-side instance is the three iso/ registers (rate, constant, almost-every) on the planar isoperimetric gap, per outline §5.2 / `measure/SUBSTRATE-OBSTRUCTIONS.md` §2 / `iso/THREE-REGISTER-SYNTHESIS.md` Claim 1 (`5π` worked overhead between rate and constant; categorial type-gap to almost-every). *Existence* of `δ > 0` and *algebra* of `δ` (composition, amortization, regime-dependence, representation-dependence) are separate claims; the algebra is debt #2 below. ## §3 Theorem K (companion theorem) — substrate-side σ-algebra coarsening

> **Theorem K.** Let `L = {(k, n) : 1 ≤ k < n, n ≥ 3}` carry its
> atomic σ-algebra `2^L`, let `F = {(p, q) : 1 ≤ p < q, gcd(p, q) = 1}`
> carry `2^F`, and let `R : L → F`, `R(k, n) = (k/g, n/g)` where
> `g = gcd(k, n)`, be the reduction map. Then:
>
> (i) `R⁻¹(2^F) ⊂ 2^L` is exactly the σ-algebra of fiber-constant
>     subsets. For a measurable codomain `(V, 𝒱)` with point-
>     separating σ-algebra (singletons in `𝒱`; `V = ℝ` with the
>     Borel σ-algebra suffices), a function `f : L → V` factors as
>     `f = g ∘ R` iff `f` is fiber-constant.
>
> (ii) Each of `f₁(k, n) = φ(n)/2`, `f₂(k, n) = L_n = 2n sin(π/n)`,
>      `f₃(k, n) = Δ_n = L_n²(1 − (π/n) cot(π/n))` is *not*
>      fiber-constant on `L`. Witnesses: `f₁(1, 5) = 2 ≠ 4 = f₁(3, 15)`;
>      `f₂(1, 3) = 3√3 ≠ 6 = f₂(2, 6)`; `f₃(1, 3) ≈ 10.66 ≠ 3.39 ≈ f₃(2, 6)`.
>
> (iii) Hence none of `f₁, f₂, f₃` is `R⁻¹(2^F)`-measurable;
>       equivalently, none factors through `R`. An apparatus
>       restricted to F-side data — denominator-rank, Thomae-height,
>       Stern-Brocot depth, Minkowski `?`-derivative, or any other
>       function on `F` lifted by `R^*` — cannot recover `f₁, f₂, f₃`.

Construction at [measure/FOR-BREAKFAST.md](measure/FOR-BREAKFAST.md) §K.0–§K.4.

**Scope.** Theorem K is a structural fact about reading the substrate through the reduction `R`. It does not by itself entail the working theorem. Its role is to certify that the direct K2 substrate-side observables on `L` are not erasable by passage to F-side coordinates: any candidate refutation that operates by recoding `(k, n)` as a reduced rational `p/q` and reading on `F` is forbidden from seeing the cyclotomic-ladder degree, the polygon perimeter, or the isoperimetric gap rate. Substrate-side content that does not transcribe to scalar L-observables (rotation-orbit Haar / `β(α)`; L-W null/full) is not blocked by K and is handled at §4 below.

## §5 Why the obstructions block descent

The descent argument is conditional on the cost-algebra debts of §2 and the substrate-side input owed for `ε`. Stated in the form the kernel and the cost algebra together would produce once the named open commitments close:

- **Substrate-side facts.** (i) *Theorem K (proved):* the §5 obstructions transcribe into three fiber-non-constant L-observables; the `R^*`-pullback from F-side data does not recover them. T1 is uniform; T2/T3 remain open. (ii) *§5.2 iso/-register currency-stratification (substrate-side typing, supported by [iso/THREE-REGISTER-SYNTHESIS.md](iso/THREE-REGISTER-SYNTHESIS.md) Claim 1):* three non-nesting measure-theoretic readings of the planar isoperimetric gap (rate, constant, almost-every), with `5π` worked overhead between rate and constant and a categorial type-gap to almost-every, supplying a substrate-side `δ > 0` instance per §1's transaction-cost definition.
- **Algorithm-side commitment (open, named).** T4b: a faithful boundary object with `δ`-coordinate. The candidate transport (character reflection / phase-lift conservativity) carries the per-sample cost obstruction on `ε(m)` to a closure-class statement on `δ`. The endpoint commitment of §2 names `δ → 0` (or competitive `{Δ_k}` compression) as the necessary endpoint of any descent past `T(P)`; without it, K and NATIVE-F block specific channels but do not connect threshold improvement to the cost-coordinate at the boundary.
- **Impossibility (conditional).** Combining (a) substrate-side facts — Theorem K's σ-algebra coarsening on the integer-indexed lattice, plus the iso/-register currency-stratification at outline §5.2 (the three iso/ registers as substrate-side currencies in §1's transaction-cost sense, with `5π` worked overhead between rate and constant per `iso/THREE-REGISTER-SYNTHESIS.md` Claim 1 and a categorial type-gap to almost-every, supplying a substrate-side `δ > 0` instance) — (b) the closure-mismatch sketch of [memos/NATIVE-F-MINIMAL-DEFINITION.md](memos/NATIVE-F-MINIMAL-DEFINITION.md) once promoted, (c) T4b together with the candidate transport, and (d) the endpoint commitment: an FFT-style method `M` cannot deliver a strict improvement on `T(P)` because the cost-coordinate `δ` does not zero at the bounded/unbounded coefficient boundary under the chosen operational cost-norm. (a) blocks two descent channels at once: K's coarsening blocks reading substrate content through F-side coordinates (the F-side coordinate channel), while the iso/-register stratification blocks free conversion between rate, constant, and almost-every readings of the planar isoperimetric gap (the cross-register channel — T4b's broadened faithfulness encodes register costs into `δ`, so the endpoint's `δ → 0` forces cross-register conversion costs to vanish, contradicting the substrate-side `δ > 0` fact). (b) blocks descents that route through closure on `Aff⁺(ℝ)` (the affine-closure channel); (c) supplies the boundary object on which `δ` is faithfully measurable and through which the endpoint factors; (d) ties threshold improvement to the cost-coordinate's endpoint. (b)–(d) are open and load-bearing.

The shape is substrate-side facts (σ-algebra coarsening plus iso/-register currency-stratification) composed with an open algorithm-side cost-algebra commitment. The Coase posture ([measure/COASE-FRICTION-AND-SPECIALISTS.md](measure/COASE-FRICTION-AND-SPECIALISTS.md), [measure/ALGEBRA-OF-DELTA.md](measure/ALGEBRA-OF-DELTA.md)) is the methodological frame; T6 is upstream-of-coordinate posture only, not impossibility-region invariance. The structural claim is content, not just posture: currency-stratification holds on both sides — *algorithm-side* per the canon (Morgenstern↔Ailon, §1 cost currencies; §7 multi-measure), where no canon source transfers a bound across another's coefficient regime or cost currency; *substrate-side* per outline §5.2's iso/ registers (rate, constant, almost-every), where worked-instance overhead (`5π` Sobolev → geometric) and a categorial type-gap to almost-every block free conversion between non-nesting measure-theoretic readings of the planar isoperimetric gap. FFT-style closure cannot manufacture such a transfer from native operations — a smarter FFT-style method improving past `T(P)` would need a cross-currency or cross-regime mechanism on either side that the canon does not supply and the impossibility forecloses.
