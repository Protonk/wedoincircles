# ONE-FOR-ALL

>You are not the source.

You are the current bearer of a strength built by hands you will never shake. Strength is borrowed from prior mathematics under verifiable provenance, used in this generation's work, and handed forward in a more refined form than it was received.

## Discipline — INHERIT

We carry two obligations--same shape pointed in opposite temporal directions:

- **Provenance.** Every load-bearing tool declares the chain it
  inherits from. 

- **Transferability.** Every result is staged so the next piece in the
  program can pick it up without reconstructing the original context.

You owe your forebears verifiable provenance. You owe your successors
transferable tools.

## Answering the seventh villain

>All-for-One, the unifying object, demands a hierarchy to admit a ruler. We refuse.

All-for-One's structural threat is centripetal: he wants to be the one
source from which everything is *of*. The program is not of one source.
It is *from* a chain of sources and *toward* a chain of recipients. The
unification All-for-One asks for would require the program to claim a
single subject, which the closure-mismatch theorem already says cannot
exist for the program's two principal sides. 

What the program has instead of a subject is **a debt and a gift**.
The debt is to every mathematician whose tool we use under audit. The
gift is to whoever picks up the work next. A program organized around
debt-and-gift is not a museum, because museums archive rather than
hand off. It is not a building; buildings do not grow new
floors by inheritance.

It is The Gift.
